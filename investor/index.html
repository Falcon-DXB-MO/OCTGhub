01

The Problem

The global OCTG market transacts $25-35 billion in tubular goods annually. Despite this scale, the engineering and commercial workflows governing tubular selection, design, procurement, stocking, and well installation remain deeply fragmented, manual, and operationally inefficient.


P.01

Fragmented Data and Catalog Silos

Engineers rely on disconnected PDF catalogs, scanned vendor manuals, and manufacturer-specific spreadsheets. Premium connection performance envelopes are buried in proprietary documents that are inconsistent, unstandardized, and unsearchable.

P.02

The Legacy Trust Barrier

Conservative engineering teams are locked into expensive, desktop-only legacy software (Landmark WELLCAT, custom Excel models). They will not abandon them without mathematical proof that any replacement produces identical, auditable results.

P.03

Manual, Unauditable Engineering Workflows

A single casing design iteration that should take minutes takes hours. Manual catalog lookups, cross-referencing of API and ISO standards, and spreadsheet-based verification produce no audit trail and poor traceability.

P.04

Commercial Inefficiency for Distributors

OCTG distributors manage pipelines in generic CRM tools with zero connection to live tubular catalogs. Sales engineers quote from memory or outdated spreadsheets. During supply disruptions, identifying qualified alternatives requires manual cross-referencing across multiple documents.

P.05

Inventory and Traceability Gaps

Generic warehouse management systems have no concept of OCTG-specific data: heat numbers, connection types, drift records, inspection certifications, or condition grading at joint level. Stock decisions are made without actionable visibility.

P.06

No Permanent As-Built OCTG Record

Once tubulars are installed, the record of what was installed — at which depth, from which heat, with which makeup torque — lives in paper tally sheets and the memory of rig personnel. This creates regulatory exposure and makes failure analysis impossible.

Key Risks and Mitigations

Enterprise sales cycle length
Focus Years 1-2 exclusively on distributors and consultants with 2-8 week cycles. Build case studies before approaching enterprise. Never depend on enterprise revenue to reach break-even.
Legacy software inertia
Parallel Validation mode directly addresses this. Engineers validate OCTG.ai against their existing models before committing. Trust is proven through mathematics, not marketing.
Third-party certification timeline
Engage certifying body (DNV, Bureau Veritas, or independent PE firm) in Month 1. Scope to API TR 5C3 / ISO 10400 mathematical methods. Budget 6 months and $50K-$150K.
Premium connection data availability
Engage Tenaris, Vallourec, and Grant Prideco for data partnership or licensing. Initial version uses available API pipe body data. Premium connection envelopes added progressively.
HITL ingestion cost and bottleneck
Charge catalog digitization fee during pilot phase to offset cost. Automate progressively as confidence-weighted extraction matures. HITL is an investment, not a permanent cost structure.
Oil price volatility
OCTG.ai reduces cost in existing workflows — it is an efficiency tool, not a capex item. Distributors continue operating regardless of oil price. Consultant demand increases in downturns as operators reduce headcount.
