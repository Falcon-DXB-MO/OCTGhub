The problem

OCTG engineering still runs on PDFs, scanned manuals, and spreadsheets.

The tubular ecosystem is fragmented across manufacturers, standards, and units — and locked into legacy desktop tools that conservative engineering teams trust but can't audit, search, or scale.

01

Fragmented data & connection silos

Pipe-body specs, premium-connection performance envelopes, and steel grades live in disconnected PDF catalogs and proprietary sheets that never reconcile.

02

The legacy trust barrier

Teams are locked into deeply trusted desktop software and customized Excel models — and rightly skeptical of any "black-box" AI touching safety-critical numbers.

03

Manual, unauditable workflows

Casing and tubing design means manual catalog lookups, hand cross-referencing of standards, and spreadsheet verification with little traceability.

04

Knowledge loss

Critical engineering judgment is buried in non-standardized documents and senior engineers' heads — vulnerable to attrition and impossible to search.

Six modules. One data record. Full lifecycle.

OCTG.ai is a connected suite built around a certified engineering core. Every tubular is cataloged, designed, centralized, sold, stocked, and permanently recorded — one continuous record from spec sheet to wellbore.
